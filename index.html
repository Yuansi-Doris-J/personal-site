Hello! My name is Doris Yuansi Jiang. I am a student at Northeastern University majoring in Data Science and Business Administration with a concentration in Supply Chain Management, along with a minor in Economics. My academic and professional experiences have helped me develop a strong interest in using data to solve business problems, evaluate market trends, and support strategic decision-making.

What excites me most about data analytics is not only the technical process of working with datasets, but also the opportunity to uncover insights that can create real impact. Through my experience as a Research Assistant in U.S. Industrial Policy and Fiscal Analysis, I have worked with multi-source federal datasets and used tools such as Python, Pandas, NumPy, SciPy, and Matplotlib to analyze long-term policy trends.

During my experience in Global Payment Growth Strategy & Operations, I explored how data-driven decision-making operates in fast-moving international business settings. I conducted market research, supported A/B testing strategies, and helped improve payment-funnel efficiency through insight-driven recommendations.

Beyond academics and internships, I am also interested in research, global markets, venture capital, and the intersection of technology and business. I enjoy learning about new industries, building practical skills, and exploring how analytical thinking can support both innovation and long-term growth.
